The First Android: A Journey Through Time

1495: Leonardo da Vinci's Mechanical Knight

While not strictly an android, this mechanical knight is considered one of the earliest designs for a humanoid robot. Leonardo's detailed sketches showed a complex system of pulleys and gears that could make the knight stand, sit, and move its arms.

1738: The Digesting Duck

Jacques de Vaucanson created this mechanical duck that could eat and appear to digest food. While not humanoid, it represented a significant step in automaton development.

1810: Friedrich Kauffman's Trumpeter

One of the first humanoid automatons capable of playing music, this mechanical trumpeter could play several military marches.

1927: Westinghouse's Herbert Televox

Often considered the first true android, Televox was an automated phone operator that could respond to voice commands and perform simple tasks.
